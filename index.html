How every part gets made.

Three machines working from common scan data. Design once in Fusion 360, fabricate across the shop.

01

3D Scan

Sub-millimeter capture of interior surfaces. Every curve, clip, and screw boss digitized.

02

CAD Design

Modeled against scan geometry in Fusion 360. Virtual fitment before cutting anything.

03

Fabricate

X1C prints ASA structure. Nova 51 engraves trim. CNC machines molds, wood, panels.

04

Test & Ship

Test-fit on our truck. Install guide in the box. It fits, or we make it right.

A family shop in Chelsea, Michigan.

Heritage Interior Systems is a product line from Dubble-O Design Co. We own a GX470, a 4Runner, and a Tundra. We got tired of choosing between ill-fitting parts from overseas and $2,000 custom work. So we bought a 3D scanner and started making parts ourselves.

We scan our trucks. Design in Fusion 360. Print on two Bambu X1Cs, cut and engrave on a Thunder Laser Nova 51, machine on a CNC router. Every part gets test-fit before it ships. If it doesn't fit, we fix it.
